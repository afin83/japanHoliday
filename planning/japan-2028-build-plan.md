# Japan 2028 — Family Trip: Itinerary + Map-First Web App Build Plan

A hand-off document for building a mobile-optimised HTML/CSS/JS itinerary page.
The page is **map-first**: a map of Japan is the home screen; every location and
travel leg is tappable and drills into detail.

---

## 1. Trip at a glance

- **Travellers:** 2 adults + 2 boys (14 and 11 at time of travel).
- **Recommended window:** depart Brisbane **~Fri 7 Jan 2028**, fly home **~Wed 19 Jan 2028** (12 nights).
  - Land back Thu 20, with a long weekend to recover before school resumes **Mon 24 Jan 2028** (QLD Term 1; confirm exact Brisbane Catholic Education date once published).
- **Why this window:** snow days land ~12–14 Jan = peak January powder in Shiga Kogen, still inside the school holidays. (The single snowiest week is the 3rd week of Jan, but that would eat into the first days back at school.)
- **Route (west → east, no backtracking):** Osaka → Matsumoto → Shiga Kogen → Tokyo → fly out of Tokyo.
- **Transport rule:** train, express bus, taxi or shuttle only — **no driving**.

## 2. Itinerary content (what the app displays)

> **All clock times are approximate**, based on current typical schedules (2028 timetables aren't published yet). Reconfirm flights, trains and opening hours closer to booking.

> **Transport key** — two kinds of rail run through this trip:
> - **Long-haul, reserved (the big between-city legs):** *Shinkansen* = bullet train (high-speed); *Ltd Exp Shinano* = limited express; plus the *Nagano ↔ Shiga express bus* (intercity coach). Reserve seats ahead — these are tagged **"— bullet train / limited express / intercity coach"** below.
> - **Local, hop-on-hop-off:** *Metro/subway* and *local train* lines (JR Yumesaki, Keiyo, Yamanote, Yurikamome, etc.) — just tap on with an IC card, no booking.

## 3. Web app concept

A single mobile-first page where the **map is the home screen**:

- Pins for every base and key stop, connected by lines in trip order.
- Each connecting line is annotated with mode + time + distance, so you can *see* the shape of the trip.
- Tap a **pin** → detail sheet (what's there, dates, how long, activities, bookings).
- Tap a **line** → transit detail (mode, duration, distance, cost).
- A segment highlight makes the current/selected leg stand out.

---

## 4. Recommended tech stack

- **Map:** [Leaflet](https://leafletjs.com/) + OpenStreetMap tiles — free, no API key, excellent on mobile, easy markers + polylines + popups. (Alternatives: Mapbox/Google need a key + billing.)
- **App:** vanilla JS + mobile-first CSS is enough; a light framework is optional.
- **Persistence:** `localStorage` for anything you tick or type (checklists, actual spend). *Works fine on your own hosted/local page — only Claude.ai's in-chat artifacts block it.*
- **Structure:** single page, no build step needed; content lives in a data file.

---

## 5. Screens / components

1. **Map home** — Leaflet map of Japan; ordered pins; annotated route lines; segment highlight; a compact "trip summary" strip (total nights, dates).
2. **Location detail** — bottom sheet / slide-up panel: name, dates, nights, day-by-day activities, durations, bookings, notes.
3. **Leg detail** — mode, duration, distance, cost, tips (e.g. "reserve seats").
4. **Packing & prep checklists** — two lists with checkboxes; prep items can carry due dates.
5. **Budget tracker** — planned vs actual by category; running total.
6. **Booking details** — per-day confirmations, times, reference numbers, links.
7. **Nav** — a small bottom bar or menu to jump between Map / Budget / Checklists / Bookings (map stays the anchor).

## 7. Persistence rules

- **Itinerary content** (locations, legs, planned budget): static in `data.js`.
- **User state** (checklist ticks, actual spend): `localStorage`, keyed by item `id`, loaded on start and saved on change.

4. **Checklists:** packing + prep with `localStorage` persistence.
5. **Budget tracker:** planned vs actual, categories, running total.
6. **Booking details:** per-day confirmations attached to locations.
7. **Polish:** mobile spacing, a winter/Japan theme, segment colour-coding, loading states.
